Ophthalmic Terminology
SPH
Sphere — main lens power. Negative (−) corrects myopia; positive (+) corrects hyperopia. In diopters (D).
CYL
Cylinder — corrects astigmatism. Usually written as negative in minus-cylinder notation.
AXIS
Orientation of the cylinder correction, 1°–180°. Only meaningful when CYL ≠ 0.
ADD
Near Addition — extra plus power for presbyopic reading. Typically +0.75 to +3.50 D.
SE
Spherical Equivalent = SPH + (CYL ÷ 2). Summarises total refractive power for CL fitting.
Prism Δ
Prismatic correction in prism diopters (Δ). Compensates for eye misalignment.
Vertex Dist.
Distance from back of lens to cornea (~12mm). Affects effective power for Rx > ±4D. Critical for CL conversion.
Prentice Rule
Prismatic Effect (Δ) = Power (D) × Decentration (cm). Used to calculate unwanted prism from off-centre lenses.
Anisometropia
Significant SE difference between eyes. >1D may cause binocular issues; >2.5D needs specialist assessment.
IOP
Intraocular Pressure. Normal range: 10–21 mmHg. Elevated IOP is a key glaucoma risk factor.
Accommodation
The eye's ability to change focus. Amplitude = max power of focus change. Declines with age (presbyopia).
Monocular PD
Per-eye pupillary distance measured from nose centre to each pupil. More accurate than total PD for lens centration.
VA (20/x)
Visual Acuity. 20/20 is normal. 20/40 means the patient must be at 20ft to see what a normal eye sees at 40ft.
OD / OS / OU
OD = Right Eye. OS = Left Eye. OU = Both Eyes.
